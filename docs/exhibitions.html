Sumschivara

MicoCosmo Festival, Capo di Ponte, Brescia, Italy
26 September - 28 September 2025

Sumschivara explores the boundary between life and death through an audiovisual and interactive installation that tells the story of the metamorphosis of a dead sumac inhabited by the fungus Schizophyllum commune.
Visitors take part in a two-phase ritual: first, the initiation, with a drink made from the fruits and fungi of the sumac; then, the immersion, through headphones and wearable devices that allow them to enter the body of the plant.
Sounds and vibrations translate the data collected from the trunk, the fungi, and the surrounding environment, making the process of degeneration and regeneration — the transformation of matter into new life — perceptible.






The Fabric of Morenica

For the closing party of Rivalta Viva Aps, Rivalta di Torino, Italy
17 May - 19 May 2025

The Fabric of Morenica is a sound work that tells the story of the material ecology of the morainic amphitheater of Rivoli-Avigliana. The piece is the result of a small, portable quantum phononic device designed to enhance the spatial and temporal perception of the person using it. This device, no longer based on outdated silicon, has an architecture dynamically shaped by the same matter as the real phenomena it explores. It is a computer capable of thinking-with-the-world, not simply simulating it—a machine that does not merely calculate, but vibrates, reacts, and resonates like what it observes. It allows us to explore reality with a gaze more attuned to its complexity.
The piece is born from a technology that goes beyond representing the world—it enables us to enter it, to access its hidden fabric: to move through it and be transformed with it.



In The Fabric of Morenica, I experimented with gate-based quantum computation and quantum concepts such as the phonon, Bose-Einstein condensate, superposition, and entanglement as both analytical methods and speculative tools for artistic creation.
As analytical methods, I used quantum computing for:
a) the spectral analysis of sound recordings,
b) the simulation of a system composed of phonons, and
c) the simulation of a Bose-Einstein condensate subjected to linear temperature variations.

Starting from a collection of my own sound recordings of the morainic amphitheater, I used spectral analysis to extract a set of frequencies describing the acoustic spectrum of the territory. These frequencies then served as initial parameters for simulating the behavior of phonons. I used the results of the phononic system to guide the selection and manipulation of audio samples through granular synthesis. The data from the Bose-Einstein condensate simulation, on the other hand, informed the musical composition process.










KA//PA//RO

With Lorenzo Peluffo
Visions in Motion, Recontemporary, Cape Town, South Africa
18 February - 23 February 2025

KA//PA//RO is an audiovisual installation that narrates the encounter between a Juniperus phoenicea grown in Piedmont and the plants inhabiting the garden of the villa that hosted the exhibition.
To envision how this encounter might have unfolded, we developed a program that simulated the event by creating a system composed of the corresponding virtual plants. As the program ran, we recorded the evolution of the system by monitoring the exchange of volatile organic compounds (VOCs)—a class of chemical compounds used by plants for communication.
The resulting data is presented in both musical and visual forms:
❧ Musically, through a soundscape incorporating electronic sounds, sonifications, and manipulated field recordings.
❧ Visually, through graphic interfaces displaying the software simulation: one animated graph, where the system’s evolution is visible, and three other screens where the system is transformed into a sonic structure.
This work is part of our ongoing research into interspecies thinking and speculative storytelling practices. Through this exploration, we develop new forms of collaboration and connection between different organisms, embracing their distinct perspectives by employing contemporary technological tools.






Gallus gallus. Diary of 10 Nights in August

With Lorenzo Peluffo
Ghëddo, Superbudda, Turin, Italy
23 October - 1 November 2024
The book is part of the Interspecies Library, New York City, NY, USA

The project unfolds as an experiment in speculative narrative, intertwining technology, bioacoustics, and futurology.
In August 2024, we recorded the sounds emitted by two red hens, Monica and Samantha, over ten consecutive nights using a portable recorder and a surveillance camera. The recordings were analyzed to identify the different types of sounds produced during sleep, tracing the sequence and tonal variations, with the aim of creating a ten-night diary.
This process led to the development of an artificial intelligence capable of delivering a revelation each morning to the human living alongside the hens. This AI functions as a digital oracle: it processes the hens’ nighttime sounds and, like a modern-day dream interpreter, transforms their memory into a narrative of the future, shared with the human.
The installation featured a book narrating the project and illustrating the ten-night diary, along with two monitors displaying footage from the surveillance camera and a visual representation of the artificial intelligence at work.






Holobiont

With Lorenzo Peluffo
Milano Music Week, Linea, Milan, Italy
19 November - 24 November 2024

Holobiont is an audiovisual installation that explores the relationship between plants and their surrounding environment. Through diagnostic sensors, AI monitors the plants’ health, capturing phytoelectrical and phytoneural activity to map behavioral patterns. Additional sensors collect data from the external environment, enabling the AI to analyze trends, predict developments, and even intervene if necessary. The plant’s responses, environmental dynamics, and AI analyses are shared with visitors as a real-time generative soundscape, accompanied by two monitors displaying the sound design process and a graphic produced with a genetic algorithm and cellular automaton—symbolizing the system’s evolution.






Study for a Sound Garden in Val Susa

With Lorenzo Peluffo
The Forte of Exilles, Turin, Italy

The installation creates a space where we foster a unique collaboration between humans, wild plants that once grew on the slopes of the Forte of Exilles, and a computer.
The computer monitors the room’s environment—tracking temperature, humidity, air pressure, light intensity, and air quality—while also gathering data from the plants in the garden. Through sensors placed on their leaves, the system reads the plants’ electrical pulses per minute and their phytoneural activity.
Inside the computer, an artificial intelligence processes this data and transforms it into sound, which serves as the auditory backdrop of the installation.






Immortal Nature

With Mariapaola Infuso
Pastis, Turin, Italy
20 June 2024

Immortal Nature is an audiovisual installation that connects a plant, to be precise an alocasia, with an artificial intelligence system. The installation explores a potential future scenario where the organic and the inorganic collaborate. Artificial intelligence simulates an ultra-innovative genetic technique that aims to speculatively modify the plant’s DNA to make it immortal.
The work explores new scenarios and new forms of power in the field of biotechnology in which the machine is able to intervene on the genetic sequences of living beings in an automated manner.
In the installation, artificial intelligence monitors both the environment in which the plant is placed and its state of health. By examining the data collected through the sensors, it generates new DNA sequences with which they replace the genes considered harmful to the plant. The two monitors on the sides of the alocasia show processes that cannot be observed with the naked eye. It is thus possible to view the vital parameters of the plant, the problems found within its organism and the solutions implemented by the machine to re-establish the ideal condition of immortality. A real-time generative soundscape based on the slow tropisms of the plant and the genetic codes produced by the machine describes the relationship between the organic and the inorganic.






Symbiotic Concert

With Lorenzo Peluffo
Spring Rolls, Lavanderia a Vapore, Turin, Italy.
26 April - 28 April 2024
L’Orto che cura, Collegno, Italy.

Holobiont is an installation that involves humans, plants and computers in the process of music creation. The collaboration between these three beings is made possible by a system that monitors the behaviour of humans and plants by means of sensors for body diagnostics and sensors for environmental monitoring. The data produced by these devices are analysed and processed by algorithms and neural networks to generate music in real time, establishing the timbres and structure of the composition based on the different behaviours observed.
Holobiont presents a reflection on the inclusion of non-human beings within human society, a topic that raises questions about how we view and represent them within our institutions and organisations. By involving humans and non-humans within an artistic process such as music through the mediation of technology, Holobiont proposes an experience in which we can enjoy a way of living based on alliance and the altruistic rediscovery of relationships.

Short Concert for Houseplants

With Fortuna De Nardo, Eugenio Nicolella and Lorenzo Peluffo
Istituto Italiano di Cultura, Madrid, Spain.
6 October - 21 October 2023

Taking inspiration from the experiments conducted by Cleve Backster, in Short Concert for Houseplants we worked on the construction of a system that allowed a plant to play, with the aim of creating a sound representation of its emotional intelligence that was perceptible to us humans.
The system consists of instruments that monitor the bioelectrical activity of the plants involved. The data resulting from monitoring is processed, transformed and analyzed to establish the pitch, duration, rhythm and timbre of the sounds generated through the computer.
For this Short Concert for Houseplants the composition was created by us humans during the process of creating sounds together with the plants.
